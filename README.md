**What is our project about?**


Streaming platforms have dramatically changed how audiences consume film and television content. Services like Netflix now track large-scale engagement data, allowing researchers to examine which titles attract the most attention and how audience behavior relates to characteristics of the films themselves. At the same time, platforms such as IMDb provide crowd-sourced ratings and production metadata that help describe how viewers evaluate films.

Streaming platforms have dramatically reshaped how audiences consume film and television content. Services like Netflix now distribute films across hundreds of countries simultaneously, creating the possibility for a single title to reach a truly global audience. As streaming platforms expand internationally, entertainment companies have increasingly invested in producing films and television shows from many different countries in hopes of generating global hits.

However, researchers and industry analysts have questioned whether global audiences actually watch the same content. According to an analysis by The Entertainment Strategy Guy, the success of international titles on streaming platforms may be overstated. While major successes like Squid Game suggest that global audiences are embracing foreign-language content, the data shows that most international titles fail to achieve widespread popularity across different regions. Instead, viewers often prefer content produced in their own language or region. This raises important questions about how globalized streaming platforms truly are and whether international films receive the same level of engagement as domestic productions.
